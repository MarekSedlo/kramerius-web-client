The document you requested cannot be freely viewed in accordance with Act no. 121/2000 (the Copyright Act). The Copyright Act allows remote access only to works whose last surviving author (including the translator, illustrator, etc.) died more than 70 years ago and which were published over 50 years ago. The works become freely available on 1st January of the year following the expiration of copyright. When the date of the authors’ death is unknown, both monographs and periodiacals are available through remote access 110 years after their publication. The duration of copyright is established by an agreement between the National Library of the Czech Republic and DILIA, collective rights association and OOA-S, Copyright Protection organisation, Association of Authors.

How can I access this document?

Registered users can access this document on computers in reading rooms in the Moravian Library in Brno (make sure to check the opening hours before visiting the library). When you log into the computer, select the icon "Digitální knihovna MZK" on the desktop, where you can access and view the full texts of the required documents. It is possible to print these documents when in the library.

What should I do if I cannot get to a reading room in the Moravian Library?

One of the options is to ask the National Library of the Czech Republic for a paper copy of the digital document, provided that they have this particular document in their own digital library If that is the case, fill in the order form Another option is to visit your nearest library and even if they do not have the title physically present, you can apply for an interlibrary loan service. For more information on the availability of the title and libraries that own it, use the central portal Knihovny.cz.
